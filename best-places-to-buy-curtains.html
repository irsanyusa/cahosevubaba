Best Places to Buy Curtains Online

Curtains can sometimes feel like an afterthought when you’re decorating a living space, especially with so many other elements to consider—but they shouldn’t be. The right curtains can really change how a room looks, especially when you find the color and fabric that work best. They are also not just for aesthetics; curtains perform useful functions year-round, from shielding the room from summer sunlight to keeping the heat inside during the winter.

Balancing form and function can be tricky, which is why it’s important to buy your curtains from a reputable retailer that knows how to deliver what you need. Some companies are the go-to for simple, stylish silhouettes, while others offer a range of fun prints and textiles. Price ranges will also vary, which means there’s something for every budget. Here is a list of the best curtain retailers so that you can find the perfect solution for your space.

What to keep in mind when buying curtains

How long should my curtains be?

It’s important to measure your windows and ceiling height before purchasing any curtains to ensure you get the right size you’re looking for. You should also consider where you will be hanging your curtains; if you’re using a tension rod within the window frame, then you need a shorter length than if you’re installing a curtain rod above the top of the frame. Most designers recommend hanging the curtains about 4-6 inches above the top of the window to create a framing effect. Another general rule is to have the hem of the curtains fall just above the floor, with a gap of about ¼ inch. This ensures that the fabric doesn’t drag along the ground or collect dust while still creating a sense of height.

What fabric or style should I buy?

Depending on where you’ll be putting the curtains, you might want different performance aspects. Consider how much light or sound a room gets and whether you want to enhance or minimize that so that you can figure out what curtains will serve you best. Many people like blackout curtains in the bedroom to ensure a good night’s sleep, particularly if the windows are east-facing, while others may want to protect light if it’s limited and focus more on the aesthetic of a sheer curtain. Outdoor spaces will require different fabrics that can withstand the elements, while households with pets or young children may favor machine-washable materials.

Once these needs have been considered, it’s time to think about the look. Everyone’s taste is different, so think about how the curtains will work with the rest of the room. Velvet can add drama and elegance, while others will focus more on the color or print design. There’s no right or wrong, just whatever looks best to you.

Do I need custom drapery?

Most retailers sell curtains in a couple of standard sizes because these work for the majority of windows, so you are probably okay to purchase ready-made products. However, do measure your curtains first to make sure. If you have particularly high ceilings or an especially large or oddly shaped window, you may need to have something made specially to fit. In these cases, custom curtains are the best solution to ensure a seamless look.

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

What is the difference between curtains and drapes?

While both curtains and drapes are pieces of fabric that cover a window, there are a couple of small differences. Drapes are usually made of heavier fabrics than curtains and sometimes feature a lining, which offers greater light-filtering functionality. Curtains are commonly cut to fit the size of the window or a few inches below, particularly in spaces like kitchens or bathrooms, while drapes hang to the ground and may even puddle on the floor. As such, drapes can be thought of as a more formal version of curtains, meant to add gravitas and anchor the room.

How much should I expect to pay for curtains?

As shown in our list of top retailers, the price range for curtains can vary significantly. If you are looking to spend on the lower side, you can spend as little as $20-50 on a pair of curtains that should do the job you need. Non-custom curtains can go up to a few hundred dollars, however, if you’re investing in premium fabrics, specialty designs, or longer lengths. For custom curtains, expect to spend at least a hundred dollars and potentially much more.

Are expensive curtains worth it?

This will really depend on your needs. If your focus is mostly on the look of a room, you can likely get a great option at a lower price point that will get the job done. This is particularly true if you want simple colors and designs. However, if you are particular about the quality of fabric for your curtains or are looking for something a little more sophisticated, it may be worth it to you to spend a bit more as these curtains should last you years. Finally, if you have an odd-shaped window or need something unique, it will look better if you pay for a custom product rather than try to cobble together different ready-made curtains. Spending a bit more for a tailored fit will result in a much cleaner look.

How do you make a room look expensive with curtains?

Taste is subjective, so there is no one color or fabric that will instantly make a room look more expensive. High-quality fabrics and softer colors can often look more expensive, but don’t feel like you have to give up your prints. Instead, focus on how the curtain fits the window and how it blends with the rest of the room. Curtains should never be too small. Instead, they should fall nearly to the ground and ideally should be hung a few inches above the window frame to create the illusion of higher ceilings. Pair curtains with quality hardware and consider adding a curtain tie or hardware tieback to upgrade the look. If in doubt, choose a more neutral color to complement the rest of the room, but a statement design can also take the look to the next level and tie the whole room together if you’re willing to go for it.
