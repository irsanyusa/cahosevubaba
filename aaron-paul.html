Aaron Paul (Actor) - On This Day

Profession: Actor

Biography: Aaron Paul is an American actor best known for his role as Jesse Pinkman in the television series Breaking Bad (2008–2013). His performance in the series earned him three Primetime Emmy Awards.

Paul's career spans television and film, with notable post-Breaking Bad work including the animated series BoJack Horseman (2014–2020) and the series Westworld (2020–2022).

Paul moved to Los Angeles soon after graduating from high school and had an early appearance on The Price Is Right. His career began with roles in music videos and commercials, leading to minor film roles and a recurring part on HBO's Big Love (2006–2011).

His critical breakthrough came with AMC's Breaking Bad, where the intended short-term character of Jesse Pinkman became central to the show's success due to Paul's chemistry with co-star Bryan Cranston.

Following Breaking Bad, Paul transitioned into films and other television roles. He took on leading roles in movies such as Need for Speed (2014) and had voice roles in BoJack Horseman, where he was also an executive producer. His television work continued with roles in The Path (2016–2018) and joining the cast of Westworld.

He reprised his role as Jesse Pinkman in El Camino: A Breaking Bad Movie (2019) and also made an appearance in Better Call Saul (2022).

Off-screen, Aaron Paul is married to Lauren Parsekian, and they have children together. He is involved with the Kind Campaign, which focuses on spreading kindness and preventing bullying. Paul and Bryan Cranston also released their own line of mezcal.
