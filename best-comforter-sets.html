How we selected the best comforter sets

To find the best comforter sets, we looked at popular bedding brands and took into account shopper reviews, material, washing instructions, and personal experience. Plus, we made sure there were plenty of options on the price spectrum so one could opt for more budget-friendly and luxury options.

Material

Material matters when ensuring a quality night’s rest. Many comforters are made of cotton and polyester, since these are both soft, plush materials. Aside from the material, you’ll want to see if the comforter is Oeko-Tex certified, which means the product has been tested against a list of 350 chemicals. The certification guarantees the comforter was made in an environmentally friendly facility, using socially responsible practices in terms of managing employees and labor practices.

Care instructions

When figuring out how to wash a comforter, look at the care instructions. Some comforters can be machine washed, while others need to be dry cleaned or spot cleaned. This varies based on the construction and fabric, but you don’t want to ruin your new comforter by tossing it in the washing machine if it won’t remain intact.

In terms of drying it, some comforters can be dried in the dryer, but others need to be air dried to maintain the material’s integrity. Be sure to read the specific instructions provided to make sure you clean and dry the comforter properly prior to use.

Price

Similar to sheets, the best comforter sets vary in price based on the material, brand, size, etc. In general, a comforter set will be more expensive than an individual comforter, as it may come with shams and decorative pillows. At the cheaper end, some options range from $41 to $100, while luxury sets can cost between $350 and $400.

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

What brand makes the best comforter sets?

This is a subjective question, depending on what you’re looking for in terms of bedding, but Parachute has some of the best comforters out there. As mentioned above, the brand’s Sateen Duvet Cover Set is made from soft Egyptian cotton and has ties to keep the duvet insert in place throughout the night. It’s on the pricier side, starting $299, but the quality is unmatched and it’ll transform your bedroom into a hotel-like space. Other notable brands and retailers include Eddie Bauer, Pottery Barn, and West Elm.

How do I know which size comforter set to buy?

The size of your comforter should be dependent on your bed size. If you have a full size mattress, then you should opt for a comforter designed for that. The same logic should be used when looking for comforters for twin, twin XL, queen, king, and California king sized mattresses.

Where should I store my comforter set?

If you’re worrying about how to store comforters, don’t: Most comforters aren’t difficult to store. Many come in bags that can be used to store them when they aren’t being used, while others can stay folded up in a linen closet. You’ll want to check with the packaging instructions and manual to see how the brand suggests storing their comforter, to ensure optimal quality.
